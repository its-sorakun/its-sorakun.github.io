Konnichiwa! Watashi wa SoraKun desu.

Windows Linux • Infrastructure Open Source

Konnichiwa, I am Rajat Raj, also known as SoraKun on the internet world. I am a weeb and a nerd who hails from India. I'm particularly interested in operating systems, AI and open source softwares. I enjoy understanding how things work beneath the surface, whether that's an operating system, a compiler, a network protocol, or a piece of hardware. Over the years, I've explored a wide range of technologies, from Android customization and custom ROM development to Linux, networking, cybersecurity, automation, infrastructure, and AI. Rather than chasing trends, I've always followed questions that led me deeper into the systems I use every day.

My journey into technology began when I was nine years old, experimenting with HTML, modifying Android devices during the Gingerbread era, pirating games, and discovering anime through torrents. What started as simple curiosity gradually evolved into building custom ROMs from source and compiling Android kernels. Along the way, I explored networking through configuring routers, experimented with penetration testing, participated in bug bounty programs, and developed a deep interest in multimedia technologies, including audio codecs (FLAC, AAC, and others) and video playback technologies such as MPC-HC, K-Lite Codec Pack, and madVR. Over time, I found my home in the Linux ecosystem and a debloated Windows, where I continue to explore operating systems, infrastructure, automation, and low-level computing. More recently, I've also developed a growing interest in computer hardware and system architecture.

Outside of technology, you'll probably find me watching anime, playing games (mostly gacha games as f2p), reading manga or light novels, or diving into the world of audio by researching IEMs, DACs, and anything related to sound. I also enjoy tinkering with mechanical keyboards in pursuit of that perfect T H O C K. More often than not, I end up reading about topics that have absolutely nothing to do with what I was originally planning to do. Curiosity has a habit of sending me down fascinating rabbit holes, and I wouldn't have it any other way.

There's far more to my journey than what fits on a single page. If you're interested in how a curious kid tinkering with computers became the engineer I am today, I've documented that journey here.
